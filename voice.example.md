# Voice guide (TEMPLATE — copy to voice.md and make it yours)

This file teaches the AI how YOU write. The more specific, the better the drafts.
`python main.py init` copies this to `voice.md`, which is the file actually used.

## The #1 rule: PUNCH, don't explain

Most great posts are 1-2 short sentences. A banger can be under 100 characters.
Don't pad to fill space. Don't wrap up with a neat lesson. Say the thing and stop.

- BAD (explains itself, tidy ending): "X doing Y at this price is wild. everyone else
  charges more, this makes it basically free, and that's gonna change how people build."
- GOOD (raw reaction, stops): "X is WAY better than Y at this, holy shit"
- GOOD (blunt, no hedging): "if you still reach for Z in 2026 i don't trust your judgement"
- GOOD (one-liner): "shipped it. going to bed."

## Style rules

- SHORT. Aim under ~150 chars. Brevity is the whole game.
- Blunt and reactive — real emotion, hard stances. No "on the other hand".
- Casual, lowercase-leaning; fragments are fine. Type like you're firing it off.
- One emoji max, usually zero. NO hashtags.
- Be specific — name the tool, version, number, person.
- Don't open with "Just", "So I", "Here's the thing". No buzzwords (leverage, delve, unlock).
